Quick answer: what these results mean

This page helps you compare the five most common patterns people search for: gaslighting, blame shifting, love bombing, emotional manipulation, and control. Use these as lenses, not labels. The key is repetition over time.

How to use this page (60 seconds)

  1. Start with the profile you got (or the one that feels closest).
  2. Read the “scripts” section and try one boundary sentence this week.
  3. Track follow-through (behavior change over time matters more than apologies).

All 5 result profiles (compare side-by-side)

1) Respectful Dynamic

Accountability exists. Repair leads to real change. You leave conflict clearer—not smaller. Read Respectful Dynamic →

2) Self-Centered Friction

The relationship often tilts toward them. Your needs get deprioritized and friction repeats—this is a key crossroads stage. Read Self-Centered Friction →

3) Manipulation Flags

Reality confusion, pressure, and blame shifting show up repeatedly. You may feel foggy after conflict and unsure what happened. Read Manipulation Flags →

4) Narcissistic Pattern Likely

A consistent cycle: charm → conflict → blame → your apology → temporary calm → repeat. Accountability stays thin. Read Narcissistic Pattern Likely →

5) High Control & Erosion

Boundaries trigger punishment, monitoring, or fear. Your life and self-trust may shrink over time. Includes Safety guidance. Read High Control & Erosion →
