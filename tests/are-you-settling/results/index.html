How to use these results (without overthinking)

Treat your profile like a mirror, not a verdict. The key question is simple: When you ask clearly, does behavior change consistently over time? A relationship can survive a rough patch. It can’t thrive on permanent vagueness.

Settling vs. a rough patch (the fast rule)

A rough patch improves with repair + follow-through. Settling feels like you keep adapting, but the baseline stays the same. Use a three-step check: (1) Ask for one measurable change, (2) Agree on what success looks like, (3) Watch behavior for 2–4 weeks. Patterns are the answer.

Choose your result profile

Each profile page includes a short explanation, what the pattern usually means, scripts you can use, and next steps based on behavior—not wishful thinking.

  • Grounded & Met
    Consistency and respect show up reliably—your needs have a place here.
  • Needs Noticed (Not Fully Met)
    Connection exists, but follow-through is inconsistent. The gap is small but persistent.
  • Quiet Settling
    Calm on the outside, lonely on the inside—your needs are shrinking to keep peace.
  • Self-Abandonment Loop
    Over-explaining, chasing, self-editing—negotiating your worth to keep connection.
  • Under-Investment Reality
    Effort may be convenience-based—plans stay vague and follow-through stays weak.

What to do after you read your profile

One clean request beats a thousand explanations.

  1. Pick one need (clarity, follow-through, consistent contact, respectful tone).
  2. Ask clearly once (short request, measurable agreement).
  3. Watch patterns for 2–4 weeks. If it stays vague, treat that as data—not a puzzle.
  4. Protect your self-respect. Don’t bargain for basics.
