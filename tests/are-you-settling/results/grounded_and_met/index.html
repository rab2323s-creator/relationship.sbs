What this profile usually means

  • You don’t feel shame for needs like consistency, reassurance, or clear plans.
  • When something goes wrong, there’s repair—not silence, punishment, or blame-shifting.
  • Effort is visible in actions (not just words), so trust builds through patterns.
  • You can be yourself without shrinking, auditioning, or over-explaining.

Quick self-check (still useful, even in healthy relationships)

Being “Grounded & Met” doesn’t mean everything is perfect. It means the overall pattern supports you. If you want a reality check, ask:

  • When life gets stressful, does effort stay reliable or drop into “convenience only”?
  • Can you name one boundary that’s respected without consequences?
  • Do you feel safe to bring up a need without managing their reaction first?

Next steps (keep the healthy pattern strong)

  1. Protect consistency: keep one small relationship ritual (a weekly check-in, a walk, a plan-confirm habit).
  2. Reinforce repair: name what went well after conflict—healthy dynamics grow through feedback.
  3. Stay anchored: a stable relationship isn’t your entire identity; keep friends, goals, and meaning outside love.
