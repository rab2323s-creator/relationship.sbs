Toxic Relationship Test: the 12 questions people avoid asking themselves

If you’ve been Googling “am I in a toxic relationship?” it’s rarely because you’re bored. It’s because something feels off: you shrink, you second-guess, you walk on eggshells, or you keep apologizing just to restore peace. This test is built to cut through confusion—fast.

What this toxic relationship quiz measures

  • Reality erosion: gaslighting, minimization, blame-shifting.
  • Control signals: isolation, monitoring, coercion, intimidation.
  • Emotional safety: whether conflict leads to repair—or punishment.
  • Effort balance: who carries the relationship day-to-day.

Why these questions start strong

Most quizzes start vague. This one starts where real people live: the moment your body tightens before you speak, the moment your needs get called “too much,” the moment the story flips and you’re suddenly the villain. Each question is scenario-based so you can recognize patterns, not just labels.

Important note

This is not a clinical diagnosis. It’s a reflection tool based on behavior patterns. If you feel unsafe, controlled, or intimidated, take that signal seriously and prioritize support.
