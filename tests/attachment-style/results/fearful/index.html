Translation: you can feel love as both comfort and danger. You crave closeness—then your system hits the brakes to protect you.

The signature pattern (push-pull)

  • Connection feels like oxygen (high craving, high intensity).
  • Then it feels risky (fear, suspicion, overwhelm, “I’m not safe”).
  • So the system flips: pursue → retreat → pursue → retreat.

How it shows up in the moments that matter

  • Texting: craving closeness, then feeling exposed—so you pull back or test.
  • Closeness: deep talks feel healing, then suddenly “too much.”
  • Conflict: strong emotion, then shutdown or detachment.
  • Trust: hyper-alert to signs, even when nothing is “wrong.”

Your 4-step stabilization plan (works because it’s structured)

  1. Slow the pace on purpose: stability beats intensity.
  2. Name the flip: “I’m in a push-pull moment. I need a reset.”
  3. Replace tests with a request: ask for one concrete behavior (timeline, check-in, reassurance).
  4. Use a repair ritual: acknowledge impact → reassure → one next step.
