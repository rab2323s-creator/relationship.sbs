Your result: Avoidant Attachment

Translation: your system protects independence under emotional pressure. When feelings get intense, your body chooses distance to breathe.

Retake the test Attachment Style Test (Deep Profile) Privacy policy

The signature pattern

  • Closeness rises → pressure rises (even if you like them).
  • Pressure rises → shutdown (short replies, distraction, “I’m fine,” emotional exit).
  • Distance restores control, but can create trust damage if there’s no return time.

How it shows up in real life

  • After a great date: you may feel a “closeness hangover” and need space.
  • During emotional talks: you can feel flooded—words disappear.
  • Texting: you may keep it practical when things feel too vulnerable.
  • Conflict: you prefer time to think, not pressure to “talk now.”

Your upgrade plan (the one that builds trust fast)

  1. Ask for space with a return time: “I’m flooded. I need 60 minutes. I’ll come back at 8:30.”
  2. Say one emotion, one need: “I feel overwhelmed. I need a slower pace.”
  3. Repair after distance: one sentence acknowledging impact changes everything.
