What these results actually measure

This test maps two forces that shape relationship behavior under stress: attachment anxiety (how fast your alarm activates) and attachment avoidance (how strongly you protect space). Most “drama” isn’t drama—it’s protection: protection from abandonment, from overwhelm, or from vulnerability.

Secure attachment

Signature: closeness feels safe, distance doesn’t feel like a threat, and repair is normal—not a power game.

  • Texting: you don’t panic over delays; you ask directly if you need clarity.
  • Conflict: you can pause and return—without punishing or disappearing.
  • Repair: you care about resolution more than winning.

Anxious attachment

Signature: uncertainty hits like danger. Your mind tries to restore safety fast—often through reassurance loops.

  • Texting: tone + timing become “signals.” Silence becomes a story.
  • Closeness: bonding is fast; you want momentum and clarity.
  • Conflict: unresolved tension feels urgent—like you can’t breathe until it’s fixed.

Avoidant attachment

Signature: emotional pressure can feel like losing yourself. Distance restores regulation—sometimes at the cost of trust.

  • Texting: you stay practical when things feel too vulnerable.
  • Closeness: you may feel a “closeness hangover” after intense connection.
  • Conflict: flooding leads to shutdown; words disappear, space becomes necessary.

Fearful-avoidant attachment

Signature: you crave closeness and fear it. Your system flips: pursue → retreat → pursue → retreat (push-pull).

  • Texting: you want contact, then feel exposed—so you pull back or test.
  • Closeness: intimacy feels healing, then suddenly “too much.”
  • Trust: hyper-alert to signs—even when nothing is objectively wrong.

Two myths that keep people stuck

  • Myth #1: “If they love me, I shouldn’t need to ask.” Reality: secure love is built on clear requests, not mind-reading.
  • Myth #2: “If I pull away, it means I don’t care.” Reality: distance can be a stress response—not a lack of feelings.
