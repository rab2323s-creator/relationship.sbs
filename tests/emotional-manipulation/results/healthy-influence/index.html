Healthy Influence — Your Boundaries Don’t Cost You Love

What This Result Means

Your answers suggest a relationship where influence is mutual, not controlling. Disagreements don’t erase your reality, and repair leads to visible effort—not confusion. This doesn’t mean everything is perfect. It means you don’t lose yourself to keep peace.

What This Usually Looks Like

  • You can name discomfort without fear of punishment.
  • Apologies are followed by changed behavior.
  • Boundaries don’t trigger guilt or withdrawal.

Common Blind Spots

  • Assuming others experience safety the same way you do.
  • Downplaying small issues until they stack up.

Helpful Scripts

  • “I appreciate how we can repair things directly.”
  • “Let’s agree on one small change and check in next week.”

Next Steps

Protect this dynamic by naming needs early, keeping repair specific, and maintaining a life outside the relationship.
