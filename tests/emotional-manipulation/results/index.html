Read This First (So You Don’t Misread Your Profile)

These profiles are about patterns and impact—not labels.

This results hub is built to answer one core question: What happens to me when I’m honest? Not what your partner “is,” not a diagnosis—just the repeatable pattern your nervous system has been living with.

In healthy influence, honesty leads to repair. In unhealthy influence, honesty leads to a cost: guilt, confusion, withdrawal, escalation, or you feeling responsible for fixing their mood.

Spot the Mechanism (A Fast Map)

When you can name the pattern, you stop blaming yourself.

  • Gaslighting-style confusion: your memory/feelings become “debatable” repeatedly.
  • Guilt-tripping: you feel like a “bad person” unless you comply.
  • DARVO: you raise a concern → they deny/attack → you end up apologizing.
  • Silent punishment: coldness/withdrawal appears after boundaries.
  • Moving goalposts: you do what was asked—then the standard changes.

5 questions that reveal the pattern

These are the kinds of questions your body has been answering for you.

  1. After I speak up, do I feel clearer—or more confused?
  2. Do I get repair, or do I get consequences? (silence, guilt, coldness, escalation)
  3. Does kindness feel consistent—or conditional?
  4. Do we solve one issue—or does it turn into a debate about me?
  5. What does honesty cost me here? (peace, dignity, closeness, self-trust)

If you want a “clean language” approach (no diagnosis), start with: Boundaries vs Control and Healthy Boundaries Script.

What to do next

Don’t “win” the debate. Protect your reality and measure follow-through.

  • Choose one boundary you can enforce consistently.
  • Use one clear request with a timeline (2–4 weeks).
  • Track behavior, not apologies.
  • Pause circular conversations that rewrite your reality.

If you share your result with someone, share the profile and the boundary you’re practicing—avoid accusations. That reduces debate traps and keeps the focus on change.
