Love language results (pick what sounds like you)

You can read first or take the test first. Most people have a close top two—especially under stress.

Words of Affirmation

Core belief: “Say it so I can feel it.” Specific praise beats grand gestures.

  • What counts: clear appreciation, reassurance, naming what you value.
  • What hurts: silence, sarcasm, vague affection.

Quality Time

Core belief: “Presence is the point.” Distraction can feel like rejection.

  • What counts: phones-down attention, planned time, real listening.
  • What hurts: multitasking, cancellations, “together but alone.”

Acts of Service

Core belief: “Love is relief.” Help = care. Follow-through is romance.

  • What counts: reliability, initiative, doing the thing you’re carrying.
  • What hurts: promises without action, being left to carry everything.

Physical Touch

Core belief: “Safety through closeness.” Touch regulates connection.

  • What counts: consistent affection, hello/goodbye hugs, hand-holding (with consent).
  • What hurts: withholding touch, affection only during sex, unwanted contact.

Receiving Gifts

Core belief: “Thoughtfulness is love.” It’s not price—it’s “you were in my mind.”

  • What counts: small meaningful tokens, remembering preferences, a story behind it.
  • What hurts: forgotten dates, random gifts, “you’re materialistic” dismissals.

Mixed / Top Two

Core belief: “Two signals land.” Your top two are close—context decides what you need most.

  • What counts: two small rituals (one from each language).
  • What hurts: “either/or” thinking and vague effort.

How to use your result (the 3-step fix)

  1. Name the signal: “What lands for me is ___.”
  2. Make it measurable: one small repeatable behavior (not “be more loving”).
  3. Translate effort: ask for what lands, and learn what lands for them too.
