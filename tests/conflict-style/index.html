Conflict style test (deep profile)

This conflict style quiz focuses on what people actually search: why you shut down, why you get defensive, and how to fight fair. Your result includes your top style, your runner‑up, and the nervous‑system triggers that make you predictable in arguments.

What this test measures

  • Your top 2 conflict styles (avoid/compete/accommodate/compromise/collaborate).
  • Modifiers like shutdown, defensiveness, or over‑apologizing.
  • Repair scripts you can use in real conversations.

Most searched conflict questions (answered)

What is my conflict style?

It’s your default way of handling disagreement—especially when your nervous system is activated.

Why do I shut down during arguments?

Often it’s flooding: your body goes into freeze/withdraw, making words hard to access.

Is shutting down stonewalling?

It can look like it. The key difference is whether you return with repair and clarity.

What are the 5 conflict styles?

Avoiding, competing, accommodating, compromising, and collaborating.

What does collaborating look like?

Curiosity, validation, one issue at a time, and ending with a concrete next step.

What does avoiding look like?

Withdrawing, delaying, going quiet, or changing topics when conflict heats up.

What does competing look like?

High directness, pushing your point, debate energy, prioritizing outcomes.

What does accommodating look like?

Keeping peace, apologizing quickly, minimizing your needs.

What does compromising look like?

Meeting in the middle and getting a workable solution fast.

Is avoiding conflict always bad?

No. Space is healthy if you return. Vanishing without repair erodes trust.

Why do I get defensive?

Defensiveness is self-protection when you feel blamed or unsafe.

How do I stop being defensive?

Validate one point first, then explain. Defend less, clarify more.

How do I stop escalating fights?

Slow your body down, lower volume, stick to one issue, and call a time-out early.

What is fair fighting?

No insults, one issue at a time, time-outs with return time, and a clear repair step.

How do we repair after conflict?

Acknowledge impact → reassure safety → agree on one behavior change.

Why do we keep having the same fight?

The surface topic changes, but the unmet need or boundary stays the same.

How do I communicate with someone who shuts down?

Offer a pause + return time, reduce intensity, and ask for one sentence of engagement.

How do I communicate with someone who escalates?

Stay calm, set tone boundaries, and ask for one concrete request instead of accusations.

Can two different conflict styles work?

Yes—if both can name their style and agree on shared rules for repair.

What’s the best conflict style quiz?

One that identifies top two styles and gives specific repair scripts, not vague advice.

Is silent treatment emotional abuse?

It can be if used to punish/control. A regulated time-out with return time is different.

What’s the difference between a time-out and avoidance?

A time-out includes a return time and commitment to repair; avoidance is indefinite.

Why do I freeze in conflict?

Freeze is a threat response. Your brain prioritizes safety over speech.

How do I stop freezing?

Regulate first (breathing, grounding), then speak in one sentence at a time.

How do I bring up issues without starting a fight?

Use one behavior + one impact + one request. No global character attacks.

How do I apologize effectively?

Name impact, take responsibility, and state the new behavior you’ll try.

Why does tone matter so much?

Tone signals safety. Many people can’t hear content if tone feels hostile.

How do I set boundaries in conflict?

Be specific, calm, and consistent. Boundaries are actions you control.

What if my partner refuses to repair?

That’s a bigger issue than style. No style works without mutual effort.

How do I handle conflict in long distance?

Use scheduled talks, time-outs with return time, and written agreements.

Why do I go for ‘logic’ in fights?

Logic can be protection. Add validation so logic doesn’t become dismissal.

Why do I apologize too much?

Over-apologizing can be a safety strategy. It stops tension but doesn’t solve needs.

How do I stop over-apologizing?

Replace automatic sorry with: ‘I hear you. Here’s what I can do.’

Why do I avoid bringing things up?

If conflict feels unsafe, your system prioritizes peace over truth.

How do I stop avoiding?

Start with small issues, use scripts, and set a short time-boxed conversation.

What’s a repair script?

A sentence that lowers threat and moves toward a solution (example: ‘I care; I’m flooded; I will return at 7.’).

Can conflict styles change?

Yes—skills, safety, and repeated repair can shift your default responses.

Is conflict healthy?

Yes—if it’s repaired. The damage comes from contempt, stonewalling, and no repair.

What’s one habit that fixes most fights?

Time-outs with return time + a weekly check-in ritual.
